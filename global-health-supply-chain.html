Key Findings

01

The Blended Average Trap — $73M Hidden

Operations were planning inventory replenishment against an 88.5% blended corporate OTD rate — a figure that masked a structural failure. Isolating Ocean freight revealed a true 84% OTD rate and 144-day average lead time, versus Air at 88% OTD and 88 days. The blended 116-day planning assumption did not exist in the physical world and was causing systematic stockouts in PEPFAR destination countries. With 99% of $1.46B in spend concentrated in just two commodity groups (ARV + HRDT), a 5% freight cost reduction recovers approximately $73M annually — but only once mode-level accountability replaces blended averages.

02

Spend Concentration Pareto

Two commodity groups — ARV antiretroviral drugs and HRDT HIV Rapid Diagnostic Tests — account for over 99% of total logistics spend across the entire $1.46B USAID SCMS network. This is the Pareto lever. Every procurement negotiation, routing decision, and OTD improvement effort should be directed at these two groups exclusively. Diffusing effort across the long tail of secondary commodities produces negligible financial return.

03

Air Charter as Strategic Insurance

Air Charter operates at 100% OTD. Despite higher per-kg cost, it is the correct routing decision for high-priority, low-weight ARV shipments during peak demand windows. The economics are straightforward when stockout risk is measured against patient health outcomes rather than unit freight cost alone. The analysis provides a clear routing rule: Air Charter for priority ARVs, Ocean restricted to high-volume base stock where OTD variance is financially tolerable.

Analytical Methodology

Python Pipeline (Pandas)

Automated ingestion and programmatic cleansing of fragmented SCMS shipment records — handling missing capture dates, non-numeric cost fields, and multi-country delivery records across Africa, Asia, and Latin America.

  • Feature-engineered delivery lead time and on-time flags from scheduled vs. actual dates
  • Standardized weight and freight cost conversions across mixed unit formats
  • Imputed NULLs for line item insurance and shipment mode classifications
  • Cost-per-kg analysis by commodity group and transport mode

Mode Isolation & OTD Decomposition

The core analytical pivot: instead of accepting the corporate blended OTD average, all shipment performance was calculated strictly by transport mode (Air Charter / Air / Ocean). This decomposition is what exposed the 84% Ocean OTD rate buried inside the 88.5% corporate figure — and made the $73M cost recovery opportunity calculable.
