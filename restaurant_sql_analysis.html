Restaurant Order Analysis with SQL

An exploratory data analysis of menu items and customer orders to identify sales trends and top-performing products.

Objective

To analyze customer behavior and item popularity by exploring and combining menu item and order detail tables.

Tools Used

SQL MySQL

Data Source

Restaurant Orders Dataset

Related Industries

Hospitality Food & Beverage Retail Analytics

Key Queries & Insights

‚ùì Question 1: How many dishes are in each category and what is the average price?

This query provides a high-level overview of the menu's composition, showing the diversity of offerings and the price points for each food category.


SELECT
    category,
    COUNT(menu_item_id) AS number_of_dishes,
    ROUND(AVG(price), 2) AS avg_dish_price
FROM menu_items
GROUP BY category;

üí° Result & Insight:

The analysis shows that Italian dishes are the most expensive on average ($16.75), while American dishes are the most affordable ($10.07). This information is crucial for menu pricing strategies.

‚ùì Question 2: How many orders had more than 12 items?

By using a subquery with a `HAVING` clause, I can identify the number of exceptionally large orders, which might represent catering or large parties.


SELECT COUNT(*) AS total_large_orders
FROM (
    SELECT
        order_id,
        COUNT(item_id) AS item_count
    FROM order_details
    GROUP BY order_id
    HAVING COUNT(item_id) > 12
) AS large_orders;

üí° Result & Insight:

There were 20 orders containing more than 12 items. While a small fraction of the 5,370 total orders, these high-volume orders are valuable and could be targeted for special promotions.

‚ùì Question 3: What were the least and most ordered items?

After joining the tables, I calculated the order count for each menu item to find the most popular (fan favorite) and least popular dishes.


-- Most ordered item
SELECT
    item_name,
    category,
    COUNT(order_details_id) AS num_orders
FROM menu_orders
GROUP BY item_name, category
ORDER BY num_orders DESC
LIMIT 1;

-- Least ordered item
SELECT
    item_name,
    category,
    COUNT(order_details_id) AS num_orders
FROM menu_orders
GROUP BY item_name, category
ORDER BY num_orders ASC
LIMIT 1;

üí° Result & Insight:

The Hamburger (American) was the most popular item with 622 orders, while the Chicken Tacos (Mexican) were the least popular with only 123 orders. This suggests an opportunity to either promote the tacos or replace them.

‚ùì Question 4: What are the details of the top 5 highest-spending orders?

This query identifies the top 5 orders by total revenue and then breaks down the composition of those orders by food category to understand what drives high-value purchases.


SELECT
    order_id,
    category,
    COUNT(item_name) AS total_items
FROM menu_orders
WHERE order_id IN (440, 2075, 1957, 330, 2675)
GROUP BY order_id, category
ORDER BY order_id, total_items DESC;

üí° Result & Insight:

The highest-spending orders are not dominated by a single category. For example, order #440 (the highest at $192.15) was primarily Italian food (8 items), but also included items from every other category. This shows that high-value customers tend to order a variety of items.

1. Initial Data Exploration

The first step involved exploring the `menu_items` and `order_details` tables independently. I ran queries to find the total number of items, price ranges, and category distributions from the menu. I also determined the date range and order volume from the order details.

2. Table Combination for Deeper Analysis

To analyze customer behavior, I combined the two tables using an `INNER JOIN` on the item ID. This created a comprehensive `menu_orders` table that linked every ordered item to its menu details, including category and price.

3. Customer Behavior Analysis

With the combined data, I focused on answering key business questions. I wrote queries to identify the most and least frequently ordered items and to calculate the total spend for each order, allowing me to pinpoint the highest-value transactions.

4. Key Findings & Insights

  • The menu has a balanced distribution of dishes across categories, but a significant price variance, with Italian dishes being the most premium.
  • The "Hamburger" is a key driver of sales volume, while "Chicken Tacos" underperform significantly.
  • High-value orders are not from a single category but are composed of a wide variety of items, indicating that top customers enjoy exploring the entire menu.

5. Recommendations

  • Optimize the Menu:
    • Prominently feature popular dishes like the Hamburger.
    • Re-evaluate underperforming items like the Chicken Tacos by adjusting the recipe, price, or replacing them.
  • Pricing Strategy:
    • Adjust pricing or portion sizes based on category performance and cost.
  • Marketing & Promotions:
    • Introduce seasonal specials or bundles, potentially featuring a mix of items from the popular Italian category.
