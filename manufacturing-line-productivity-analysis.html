Manufacturing Line Productivity Analysis

An Excel-based analysis to identify the root causes of production downtime and provide data-driven recommendations.

Objectives

  • Transform and merge multiple data sources into a single dataset.
  • Identify the primary drivers of downtime (reasons, products, operators).
  • Provide data-driven recommendations to improve line efficiency.

Tools Used

Excel Power Query

Data Source

Manufacturing Dataset

Internal dataset from a beverage bottling line.

Related Industries

Manufacturing Supply Chain Operations Management

About This Project

ETL - Data Cleaning & Transformation (Power Query)

  • Unpivoting Downtime Data: Transformed 12 separate columns for downtime factors into two, creating a "tall" table for easier aggregation.
  • Merging Data Sources: Merged downtime data with downtime factors and line productivity data to create a single, comprehensive fact table.
  • Handling Complex Calculations: Created custom columns to handle overnight shifts and calculate an efficiency metric.

Analysis & Visualization (PivotTables & Charts)

Insights:

  • A Pareto Chart revealed that the top 3 downtime reasons (Machine Adjustment, Machine Failure, and Inventory Shortage) were responsible for the vast majority of lost time.
  • The CO-600 product was identified as the single largest source of downtime.
  • Downtime was not random, but clustered around peak production hours.

Operator Performance:

  • Analysis separated downtime into "Operator Error" and "Systemic Issues" to provide a fair assessment of performance.
  • Identified targeted training needs for specific tasks (e.g., Charlie with machine adjustments, Mac with batch changes).

Strategic Recommendations

  • Launch a preventative maintenance review, focusing on the equipment used for the CO-600 line.
  • Investigate the supply chain to address the root cause of inventory shortages.
  • Implement targeted operator training to address specific, predictable issues.
