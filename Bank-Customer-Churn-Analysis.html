Bank Customer Churn: Predictive Analysis

A machine learning pipeline to predict customer churn and inform targeted retention strategies.

Objective

A major bank faced a significant 20% customer churn rate, leading to substantial revenue loss. The bank's existing retention efforts were reactive and ineffective, prompting this project to create a predictive tool. The goal was to anticipate customer departures, allowing for timely and effective retention efforts.

Analysis Workflow

The project followed a comprehensive analytical workflow to ensure the final solution was both statistically sound and aligned with business goals.

  • Data Preparation: The initial dataset was cleaned and prepared for analysis. This involved handling missing values, correcting data types, and ensuring overall data integrity.
  • Feature Creation: To uncover deeper patterns, new features were created from the existing data. Key features included BalanceSalaryRatio to measure financial health and an interaction term, Germany_X_Balance, to test a hypothesis that the account balance of German customers had a unique relationship with their likelihood to churn.
  • Modeling: Several classification algorithms were evaluated, with Random Forest and Gradient Boosting emerging as the top performers. To handle the class imbalance (more non-churners than churners), the SMOTE technique was integrated into a Pipeline with the Gradient Boosting model.
  • Model Optimization: The top models underwent extensive hyperparameter tuning using RandomizedSearchCV and GridSearchCV to find their most effective settings.
  • Threshold Tuning: A final analysis was performed on the champion model to select a decision threshold that best met the business goal of capturing the most potential churners. A threshold of 0.45 was chosen, as it maximized churn detection while maintaining a precision rate above 50%.

Final Model Performance

The final, optimized Gradient Boosting model was selected as the champion. At the chosen 0.45 threshold, its performance on unseen test data was:

  • Recall: 0.74 (Successfully identifies 74% of all customers who will actually churn)
  • Precision: 0.52 (When the model flags a customer, it is correct 52% of the time)

The Key Drivers of Churn

The model revealed the specific factors that most influence a customer's decision to leave:

  • Age: Overwhelmingly the most important predictor.
  • Number of Products: A direct measure of a customer's integration with the bank.
  • Has Credit Card: A critical indicator of being anchored in the bank's ecosystem.
  • Is Active Member: A powerful behavioral signal of engagement.
  • The German Customer's Balance: The feature created for this analysis proved to be a top driver, confirming our hypothesis.

Profile of a Customer Likely to Churn

These findings allow us to paint a clear picture of a customer who shows patterns of leaving: A typical customer who is likely to churn is heavily defined by their Age. They are weakly integrated into the bank, holding a low Number of Products and often not possessing a bank-issued Credit Card. They are frequently inactive. Furthermore, if the customer is from Germany, their account balance becomes a uniquely critical factor in their likelihood to churn.

Recommendations

Based on the findings from the analysis, we recommend the following strategies:

    Launch an "Ecosystem Deepening" Campaign:

    Observation: Low product count (#2) and no credit card (#3) are top churn drivers.

    Action: Offer customers with only one product a pre-approved credit card with a bonus offer. This single action addresses two of the top three churn drivers.


    Implement an Automated "Activity Nudge":

    Observation: Inactivity (#4) is a major red flag.

    Action: Trigger an automated communication to customers who have been inactive for a set period, offering an incentive to re-engage with their account.


    Create a Specialized German Retention Desk:

    Observation: The financial profile of German customers is a unique churn signal.

    Action: Empower a specialized team to contact German customers identified by the model with tailored financial advice or competitive rates on their deposits.
